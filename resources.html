View-Master Resources

Dave Machin's View-Master Database

My personal view-master collection database. Searcheable, with digitized images of every individual picture on each reel. Over 1000 reels available! Includes slide show viewer for all the pictures. Powered by Air-Table and includes access to the entire database.

View Master Travels

My youtube channel where I visit locations from View Master reels and try and replicate the pictures. Learn some history about the locations as well.

What's My View Master Worth

Some starting information to figure out what your View Master collection might be worth.

The Vintage 3D Viewer Collection

The Vintage 3D Viewer collection visually highlights the beauty and creativity of stereoscope design, particularly during the mid-century period.

Collecting View-Master Viewers and Reels

Rebecca Kilbreath's Blog post about collecting view-masters.

The Animated View-Master Website

This website will show you the world of View-Master in an animated style. See the viewers in a 360-view, the history, the reels, the envelopes and look around in the View-Master Shop. Enjoy!

Chakka-Chhh: The Hidden History of View-Master

Mental Floss article on the history of view-master.

Single Reel Variations Database

The aim of this website is to make the most comprehensive and complete list of known variations within the same reel variety.

View-Master Database

As there was no official list of titles, this View-Master Database is an attempt to build a complete list of every individual reel, set, and their variants known to have been issued. It is hoped that the site will be of value to collectors and can continue to be expanded in the future.

View-Master Reels and Packets - A Collector's Guide

This three-volume collector's guide is a historical overview of View-Master Reels and Packets since its introduction. It is an absolute necessity for the View-Master collector in the world, written by Harry zur Kleinsmiede, who has been collecting View-Master Memorabilia longer than anyone else in the world, starting in 1952 when he was five years old.

A list of stereo-reels produced in the former Czechoslovakia (mainly by the Meopta Company), between 1962 and 1985 and also an overview of accessories, called the "Meopta stereo program".

Airtable

Airtable is the database tool used to create this web site. It has a very simple interface to load your collection and an API you can use to write a web-based front end as well.

BatchCrop

Batch crop is the tool used to automatically crop all the images used in this database.
